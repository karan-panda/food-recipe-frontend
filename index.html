Poha: A Taste of India's Breakfast Heritage

"Start your day with a burst of flavors and a touch of tradition - try our lip-smacking Poha recipe, the perfect harmony of flattened rice, veggies, and spices!"

~ Chef Karan Panda

List of Incredients

Quantity Ingredient
2 cups Poha (flattened rice)
1 medium-sized Onion (finely chopped)
1 medium Potato (peeled and diced)
1/2 cup Peas (fresh or frozen)
1/4 cup Raw peanuts (optional)
2-3 Green chilies (finely chopped)
1 teaspoon Mustard seeds
1 teaspoon Cumin seeds
8-10 Curry leaves
1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder
1/4 cup Grated coconut (optional)
2 tablespoons Oil (preferably vegetable oil)
Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Rinse the poha in a colander under running water for a few seconds. Let it sit for 5 minutes to soften. Once softened, fluff it gently with a fork and set it aside.
  2. In a large flat-bottomed pan or kadai, heat oil over medium heat.
  3. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds to the hot oil. Let them splutter.
  4. Add the raw peanuts (if using) and sauté until they turn slightly golden.
  5. Add the chopped onions and green chilies. Sauté until the onions become translucent.
  6. Add the diced potatoes and peas. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the potatoes are tender.
  7. Sprinkle turmeric powder and salt over the mixture. Stir to coat everything evenly.
  8. Add the curry leaves and cook for another minute.
  9. Lower the heat and add the rinsed poha to the pan. Gently mix everything together until the poha is well combined with the vegetables.
  10. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring occasionally, to ensure all the flavors meld together.
  11. Optional: Add grated coconut on top for an extra layer of flavor and texture.
  12. Remove from heat and serve hot with a garnish of fresh coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice.
